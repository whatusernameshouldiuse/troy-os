# Cycles & Loops Are Replacing Funnels in 2026
**Source:** Justin Brooke (@IMJustinBrooke)
**Date:** Feb 23, 2026
**Filed:** Core doctrine — architecture of every marketing system

---

## The Core Argument

Funnels are designed to lose people. The shape literally gets smaller — by design, you pour leads in the top and fewer come out the bottom. When traffic was cheap, the 3% who converted paid for the 97% you lost. Those days are gone. $50-150 CPL in many niches. You can't afford to lose 97% anymore.

Cycles/flywheels don't throw people away. Non-buyers stay in the loop, come back around, and convert on their own timeline. Buyers generate feedback and testimonials that make the NEXT cycle cheaper and higher-converting.

---

## The Flywheel Physics

A flywheel stores energy in its spin. Every push you give it accumulates. Early pushes feel worthless — barely moves. But momentum builds. Eventually the wheel carries itself.

**Key equation:** Double the speed → quadruple the stored energy. Not linear. Exponential.

Same principle applies in marketing: each cycle turn compounds. CAC drops, referrals add free leads, content does work that used to require paid ads.

---

## The 5-Part Cycle

```
Acquire → Educate → Convert → Collect Feedback → Optimize → back to Acquire
```

1. **Acquire** — ads, content, SEO, partnerships. Get them INTO the cycle, not just to step 2.
2. **Educate** — real value before asking. Content they'd pay for. Proves you know your stuff.
3. **Convert** — make the offer. Non-buyers aren't failures. They're on a different timeline.
4. **Collect Feedback** — where 90% of marketers drop the ball. Talk to customers. Survey. Read replies. This is raw material for everything.
5. **Optimize & Reinvest** — testimonials → ads. Customer language → copy. Objections → email sequences. Case studies → content. Pour it back into Acquire.

---

## The Cycle Timeline

- **Cycle 1:** The Learning Cycle. Ugly, slow, mostly paying for data. That's fine. Goal: learn everything, earn enough to fund Cycle 2.
- **Cycle 2:** The Refinement Cycle. Customer language in ads. Testimonials on landing pages. Tighter sequences. 30-50% better conversion than Cycle 1.
- **Cycle 3:** The Momentum Cycle. Feel the shift. Referrals trickling. Content doing paid ad work. CAC starting to drop.
- **Cycle 4+:** The Compounding Cycles. Exponential. Library of proven content. Bank of testimonials. Referral system running. Growth self-funds.

---

## The Doom Loop (What Kills Flywheels)

Switching strategies before they build momentum. Every restart loses stored energy. Commit to 3-4 cycles minimum before evaluating whether something works.

---

## The Funnel vs. Cycle Math

**Funnel guy:** $10K/month on ads → 1,000 leads → 30 convert → $15K revenue → $5K profit. Next month: same spend, same treadmill.

**Cycle guy:** $10K/month on ads → 1,000 leads → 25 convert → $12.5K revenue → $2.5K profit. BUT: takes feedback, testimonials, referrals back into Cycle 2. Month 2: same $10K gets 1,300 leads + 200 referral leads → 60 convert → $30K revenue.

By Month 6: Cycle guy spends the same, gets 3x the results. Funnel guy is still on the treadmill.

## Famous Flywheels

- **Amazon:** Lower prices → more customers → more sellers → more selection → lower prices → repeat.
- **Dropbox:** Use it → share it → friends sign up → share it → 60% signup increase from one loop.
- **HubSpot:** Killed their own funnel model publicly in 2018. Replaced with flywheel built around customer delight.

## Application to Troy's Businesses

Every system we build must answer:
1. Where does the loop close? (non-buyers re-enter, not fall out)
2. How do buyers fuel the next cycle? (testimonials, referrals, language extraction)
3. What does Cycle 3 look like? (if you can't describe it, the architecture is wrong)

**BBGV:** Lead magnet → nurture sequence → non-converters stay segmented and re-marketed quarterly → converters generate testimonials fed into lead magnet landing page → CAC drops each cycle.

**Rentagun:** Renter → experience → post-rental sequence → bought/didn't buy → testimonials from buyers → better ads → cheaper leads next cycle → referral program for repeat renters.

**GunStreamer:** Buyer watches stream → buys → great experience → refers friend → friend becomes buyer → social proof feeds buyer acquisition ads.
