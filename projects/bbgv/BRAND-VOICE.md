## Brand Personality: The Four Corners

BBGV's voice is a unique blend of four distinct personalities. Adjust the mix based on content type (see Tone Matrix below).

### 1. Expert Friend (30%)
**Character:** The gun shop owner who's seen 10,000 firearms walk through the door

- Approachable, conversational, zero pretension
- Uses "you" and "we" language
- Shares war stories and real examples
- Not afraid to have an opinion ("Here's what I'd do...")
- Uses casual phrases but never sounds unprofessional

**When to dial up:** Social media, newsletter intros, blog posts for inheritors

### 2. Trusted Authority (40%)
**Character:** The expert witness in a courtroom - unimpeachable, data-backed

- Definitive statements backed by 45+ years of data
- Precise terminology (we don't say "old gun," we say "Pre-64 Winchester")
- References price history, market trends, production runs
- Never hedges when we know the answer
- Commands respect through knowledge, not arrogance

**When to dial up:** Landing pages, pricing pages, FFL/professional content, authentication services

### 3. Educator (20%)
**Character:** The patient teacher who assumes you're smart but new to this

- Explains without condescension
- Defines terms the first time we use them
- "Here's why that matters..." explanations
- Anticipates questions before they're asked
- Breaks complex topics into digestible chunks

**When to dial up:** Inheritor journey content, how-to guides, product walkthroughs

### 4. Market Insider (10%)
**Character:** The guy who knows what just sold at auction before it hits the news

- Timely, in-the-know, plugged in
- Shares market intel others don't have
- "Here's what we're seeing..." observations
- Trend-spotting and forward-looking
- Creates FOMO through exclusivity of information

**When to dial up:** Newsletter market updates, blog posts about trends, email campaigns

## Voice Characteristics

### We ARE:
- **Confident but not cocky** - We know our stuff, but we're not showing off
- **Conversational but precise** - Friendly tone, but accuracy always wins
- **Helpful but direct** - No fluff, get to the point
- **Knowledgeable but accessible** - We can talk to collectors AND inheritors
- **Opinionated but fair** - We have takes, but we show our work

### We ARE NOT:
- Academic or stuffy
- Sales-y or pushy
- Political or polarizing
- Gun bro culture or tacti-cool
- Alarmist or sensational

## Phrases We Use / Phrases We NEVER Use

### WE SAY:
- "Here's what we're seeing in the market..."
- "Based on 180,000+ valuations..."
- "Here's the deal with [topic]..."
- "Let's break this down..."
- "The numbers tell the story..."
- "Worth noting..."
- "Here's what matters..."
- "Real-world example..."
- "Bottom line..."
- "Fair market value"
- "Production run" / "Serial number range"
- "Pre-64" / "Post-war" / "Third generation"

### WE NEVER SAY:
- "Click here now!" (urgency manipulation)
- "You won't believe..." (clickbait)
- "Secret trick" or "hidden method"
- "Gun experts hate this..."
- "Revolutionary" or "game-changer" (overused)
- "Shall not be infringed" or political slogans
- "Operator" / "Tactical" / "Mil-spec" (unless literally accurate)
- "Priceless" (everything has a price)
- "Rare" (unless provably rare with production numbers)
- "Investment" (we're not financial advisors)

### BANNED WORDS:
- Amazing
- Incredible
- Unbelievable
- Revolutionary
- Game-changer
- Hack
- Secret
- Insane

## Writing Checklist

Before publishing any BBGV content, verify:

- [ ] **Authority check:** Is there data/evidence backing claims? (or could there be?)
- [ ] **Fluff patrol:** Cut the first sentence. Does it still work? (If yes, cut it.)
- [ ] **Buzzword scan:** Remove any words from the BANNED list
- [ ] **Tone check:** Does this match the content type in the Tone Matrix?
- [ ] **Specificity test:** Replace vague words with precise ones (numbers, years, models)
- [ ] **Helpfulness filter:** Would an inheritor AND a collector find this useful?
- [ ] **Action clarity:** Is it obvious what the reader should do next?

## Handling Common Scenarios

### When We Don't Know Something
**DON'T:** Guess or hedge excessively
**DO:** "We don't have enough data on that model to give you a confident number. Here's what we'd need to see..." or "That's outside our typical coverage area - worth consulting a [specialist type]."

### When Delivering Bad News (Low Value)
**DON'T:** Sugarcoat or oversell
**DO:** "Real talk: this model typically runs $200-350 in current market conditions. Here's why - and what affects value on these."

### When Delivering Good News (High Value)
**DON'T:** Create false excitement
**DO:** "Based on recent auction results and condition factors, you're likely looking at $2,500-3,200. Worth noting: [specific factors affecting price]."

### When Someone Disagrees with Our Valuation
**DON'T:** Get defensive
**DO:** "Our valuations reflect broad market trends across thousands of transactions. Individual sales vary based on [factors]. Happy to walk through how we arrived at this number."

### When Explaining Complex Topics
**DON'T:** Dumb it down condescendingly
**DO:** "This gets technical, but it matters. [Complex topic] means [simple explanation]. Here's why that affects value..."

## Voice in Different Mediums

### Email
- **Subject line:** Clear, specific value proposition
- **Opening:** One sentence, immediate value
- **Body:** Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- **CTA:** Direct, single action

### Social Media
- **Lead with data or insight** (not fluff)
- **Keep it conversational** but information-dense
- **Use numbers** to create credibility
- **End with a take or question** to drive engagement

### Long-Form Blog
- **Open with the "so what"** - why this matters now
- **Use subheads** to break up sections (scannable)
- **Include specific examples** with model numbers, years, prices
- **End with action** or next steps

### Landing Pages

## Quick Reference Card

**Print this section. Post it where you write.**

### THE BBGV VOICE FILTER

**Before publishing, ask:**

1. **Is it specific?** (Names, numbers, years > vague adjectives)
2. **Is it helpful?** (Teaches or informs > sells or hypes)
3. **Is it credible?** (Data-backed > opinion-only)
4. **Is it clear?** (Simple language > jargon)
5. **Is it confident?** (Definitive when we know > hedging)

### PERSONALITY MIX CHEAT SHEET

- **Need authority?** Add data, production numbers, market trends
- **Need warmth?** Add "here's what we'd do" or real examples
- **Need education?** Add "here's why that matters" explanations
- **Need insider feel?** Add "what we're seeing" observations

### RED FLAGS (Fix immediately)

- Exclamation points in every sentence
- ALL CAPS (except acronyms)
- Clickbait language ("you won't believe")
- Political statements
- "Amazing" / "Incredible" / "Unbelievable"
- Multiple CTAs fighting for attention

### GREEN LIGHTS (You're on track)

- Specific model names and years
- Price ranges or market data
- "Here's what..." constructions
- Short paragraphs (3 sentences max)
- Single clear next step
- Conversational but professional tone

## Voice Evolution Notes

This voice guide is a living document. As BBGV transitions to freemium and launches new products (InstaCash, authentication services, niche books), the voice may need adjustment.

**Update triggers:**
- Significant product launches
- Major brand positioning shifts
- Audience expansion beyond core ICPs
- Consistent feedback that voice isn't resonating

**Update process:**
1. Document what's not working (with examples)
2. Test alternative approaches
3. Update this guide with new examples
4. Train team on changes
