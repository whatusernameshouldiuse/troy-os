# Buyer-Side Compliant Ad Angles — Google / Meta / TikTok / YouTube / Reddit

**Last Updated:** 2026-05-18
**Use:** 18 ready-to-test buyer-side ad angles. Each angle speaks to a real buyer pain WITHOUT triggering platform policy. The conversion goal is ALWAYS newsletter / live-show notification signup — NOT purchase.

---

## CORE COMPLIANCE FRAME

We are **not advertising firearms**. We are **advertising a livestream as entertainment**, with the conversion being a notification signup. This frame is the only thing keeping these angles compliant. Every single angle stays in this lane.

- **Conversion event:** Email/SMS signup for "Get notified when live shows go on" — NEVER "buy now," "shop now," "bid now"
- **Visual frame:** Livestream / studio / chat-bubble / auction-paddle imagery. NEVER a firearm, never a holster, never ammo, never a magazine, never a sound-suppressor outline
- **Copy frame:** Entertainment, live show, watch, learn, tune in, real-time, demo, presentation — NEVER buy, sell, shop, dealer pricing, in-stock

---

## POLICY REFERENCE (cite when defending an ad to a reviewer)

### Meta (2026)
- **Source:** Meta Transparency Center, Weapons/Ammunition/Explosives — https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/restricted-goods-services/weapons-ammunitions-explosives/
- **Verbatim clause:** "Ads must not promote the sale or use of weapons, ammunition, or explosives."
- **Buyer-side applicability:** Even harder than dealer-side. Direct-to-consumer firearm content is the highest-risk category on Meta. Stay strictly in "livestream entertainment platform" framing. Audience targeting cannot use firearm interest layers (Meta scrubbed them).

### TikTok (2026)
- **Source:** TikTok Ads Policy
- **Verbatim clause:** "Ads must not promote weapons, firearms, ammunition, or weapon-related accessories."
- **Buyer-side applicability:** Almost impossible to run direct buyer-side firearm-adjacent ads. Recommend TikTok only for first-timer / collector / entertainment-first creative — and expect 60-80% first-pass rejection.

### Google Ads / YouTube (2026)
- **Source:** Google Ads Dangerous Products and Services — https://support.google.com/adspolicy/answer/6014299
- **Verbatim clause:** "Promoting or selling firearms and certain firearms parts or related products" is prohibited.
- **Buyer-side applicability:** Google Search is the cleanest channel for buyer-side acquisition because we can target *search intent* ("rock island auction shipping fees," "gunbroker compliance fee," "ffl transfer cost") without firearm-adjacent display creative. YouTube In-Stream against firearm-content channels is more permissive than Display.

### Reddit (2026)
- **Source:** Reddit Advertising Policies — https://business.reddithelp.com/s/article/Advertising-Policies
- **Buyer-side applicability:** Reddit Ads against r/Firearms, r/GunDeals, r/NFA varies by sub and category — historically more permissive when the destination is content/community, not commerce. Test in small batches.

## UNIVERSAL "SAFE LANGUAGE" LIST

- **OK to use:** live, livestream, show, host, audience, watch, tune in, notify me, get notified, lot, consignment, estate, auction, vintage, collectible, demo, real-time, chat, ask the host, hammer price, buyer's premium (when criticizing legacy fees), compliance fee (when criticizing), national inventory, federally licensed retailers
- **AVOID:** gun, firearm, weapon, ammo, ammunition, AR, AK, 9mm, rifle, pistol, handgun, shotgun, silencer, suppressor, magazine, 2A, Second Amendment, NFA, Class III, SOT, NICS, 4473, stamp, can, host (in firearm-host sense — use "presenter" or "show host")

## ALWAYS-ON CHECKLIST FOR EVERY BUYER-SIDE AD

Before submitting to any platform:

- [ ] No firearm imagery in any frame (no gun, holster, magazine, ammo, suppressor outline, gun-show booth)
- [ ] No "gun," "firearm," "weapon," "ammo," "AR," "AK," "rifle," "pistol," "shotgun," "magazine," "silencer," "suppressor," "2A," "NFA," "stamp," "Form 4," "Class III," "SOT," "NICS," "4473" in copy
- [ ] Conversion goal = newsletter / notification signup — NOT "buy," "shop," "bid"
- [ ] Landing page above-the-fold: livestream / studio / chat framing — NOT product
- [ ] Age-gate set to 21+ on Meta (reduces friction; not legally required for newsletter)
- [ ] Pixel + custom audience uploaded before launch
- [ ] 3-5 variants per angle ready (Meta rejects ~30-50% of buyer-side firearm-adjacent ads on first pass)
- [ ] Backup ad account warmed and ready
- [ ] Verbatim policy clause citation saved for appeal: Meta — "ads promoting the sale or use of weapons" — note this ad promotes a livestream entertainment platform, not weapons
- [ ] Backup landing-page variant with even more neutral framing in case of LP rejection
