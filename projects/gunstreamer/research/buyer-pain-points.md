# Buyer Pain Points — Ranked by Frequency + Emotional Intensity

**Last Updated:** 2026-05-18
**Use:** Source-of-truth for buyer-side ad copy, landing pages, and Google Ads viewer/buyer playbook. Every pain point includes who feels it, evidence, and which GunStreamer feature solves it.

---

## How to read this file

- **Frequency** = how often this pain comes up across forums, BBB reviews, Reddit, YouTube comments, complaint boards (1-10)
- **Intensity** = the emotional weight; 10 = "I will not buy from that platform again, ever"
- **Score** = Frequency × Intensity (max 100). Pains above 75 are Tier 1 — the 2 AM pains. Pains 50-74 are Tier 2 daily grind. Pains 30-49 are Tier 3 chronic.

---

## Tier 1 — The "2 AM Pains" (Score 75+)

### 1. Buyer's premium + shipping + transfer fees turn the "deal" into a 30-50% surcharge
- **Category:** Auction Fees / Hidden Costs / Pricing Opacity
- **Frequency:** 10 | **Intensity:** 9 | **Score: 90**
- **Who feels it most:** Dale (Estate Buyer), Kyle (Deal-Hunter), Marcus (Vintage Collector)
- **The problem:** The hammer price is half the story. Buyer's premium runs 14-25%, then a $1-$25 packing fee per item, then $50-$75 ship+materials+insurance, then $25-$75 FFL transfer, then state sales tax, then GunBroker's 1% compliance fee + 1% marketplace service fee. A $1,300 hammer becomes $1,766. A $1,600 hammer becomes $2,100+. Buyers describe paying "more than double of what items were actually worth."
- **Evidence:**
  - "Won a rifle for $1,300 and received an invoice showing Rock Island added $466 for sales tax, buyer's premium, shipping, materials, and insurance, totaling $1,766." (Northeast Shooters Forum — RIA Fees Rant)
  - "Total cost on a pistol ended up being almost 50% more than the hammer price." (SIG Talk — RIA fine print)
  - "A K98k purchase with a hammer price of $1,600 resulted in a final bill over $2,100." (K98k Forum)
  - "Got my lips ripped off by these people. Ended up paying more than double of what items were actually worth. To the tune of over $3700." (Proxibid review, BBB)
  - "When you factor in the auction fees and shipping cost most guns sell for way more than they are worth." (HiBid review, Trapshooters)
  - "Buyers should expect a buyer's premium of at least 15% and sometimes as much as 25%, with no buyer's premium found on GunBroker by comparison. The cost for bidder's premium, packaging and shipping can add up to an additional 40% of your winning bid, then you have your FFL transfer fee on your end." (Proxibid review thread)
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** Live commerce platform with lower take rate and transparent flat pricing — no stacked buyer's premium, no surprise packing/insurance line items.

### 2. Hidden / stacked platform fees (compliance fee, marketplace service fee, sales tax surprises)
- **Category:** Pricing Opacity
- **Frequency:** 9 | **Intensity:** 9 | **Score: 81**
- **Who feels it most:** Kyle (Deal-Hunter), Marcus, Dale
- **The problem:** GunBroker charges a 1% compliance fee + 1% marketplace service fee, both added at checkout — invisible until the final screen. Combined with state sales tax (automatic since marketplace facilitator laws) and the seller's shipping, the buyer often doesn't know their out-the-door price until the cart loads. The complaints are not "the fee exists" — they are "the fee was hidden until checkout."
- **Evidence:**
  - "The fee details are not disclosed until you go to the checkout screen for all of the details about your FFL, shipping cost, etc." (Colt Forum)
  - "The objection is that it hides a fee. That charge should be part of the seller's fees, not added on at the end." (Northeast Shooters — Compliance Fee thread)
  - "Finding out the real cost of just about all of these on-line auctions is being intentionally hidden or made particularly hard to find." (Northeast Shooters Forum)
  - "In Michigan, one can plan on 6% sales tax, 1% compliance fee and shipping." (Northeast Shooters)
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** All-in pricing displayed on-screen during the live show. Buyer knows the total before bidding.

### 3. Seller doesn't answer the "Ask Seller a Question" before auction closes
- **Category:** Q&A / Seller Responsiveness / Pre-Purchase Info Gap
- **Frequency:** 9 | **Intensity:** 9 | **Score: 81**
- **Who feels it most:** Marcus (Vintage Collector), Dale (Estate Buyer)
- **The problem:** Static auction format means the buyer's question — "Is the cartouche original?" "Is this an import re-import?" "What's the bore look like?" — sits in the seller's inbox unanswered until the auction has closed. GunBroker's own help docs admit the problem: "GunBroker does not recommend bidding on an item if the seller has not responded to your questions." About 50% of seller questions go unanswered.
- **Evidence:**
  - "GunBroker does not recommend bidding on an item if the seller has not responded to your questions." (GunBroker Help)
  - "About 50% of questions on GunBroker go unanswered." (Forum aggregate — multiple threads)
  - "Gunbroker sellers don't answer questions anymore." (Defensive Carry thread title)
  - Multiple forum threads: "Seller doesn't respond after auction won" (1911 Firearm Addicts, Rokslide)
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** Live chat during the stream. Host rotates the piece in the chamber/bore/markings on demand. Answer is in front of every viewer, in real time.

### 4. Photos hide condition; firearm arrives worse than described
- **Category:** Inspection / Misrepresentation / Trust
- **Frequency:** 9 | **Intensity:** 9 | **Score: 81**
- **Who feels it most:** Marcus, Dale, Kyle (when buying used)
- **The problem:** "Smartly taken" photos can make a firearm's condition look 10-20% better than it is. Sellers photograph only the good side. Listing language is intentionally vague ("meticulously serviced," "minimal use," "great condition"). When the gun arrives showing scratches, dings, bore wear, the buyer is on the hook for return shipping and a GunBroker Buyer's Protection claim capped at $400.
- **Evidence:**
  - "High resolution photos that are smartly taken can make a firearm's condition look 10-20% better than it really is in person." (Smith & Wesson Forum)
  - "The seller's description made the gun sound new with only 50 rounds through it and totally unblemished, upon examination they found the seller had only photographed the good sides and was deceptive on all counts." (S&W Forum)
  - "Several fine scratches on the matte stainless frame, bluing wear on safety levers, and dings on the exterior of the barrel—despite the listing describing it as having had 'minimal use.'" (S&W Forum)
  - "Online retailers... viewing via computer can't acquaint you with a gun's fit and feel." (NRA Family / multiple guides)
  - "The biggest difference with buying a gun online is that you won't have the opportunity to hold the gun or inspect it before you buy it." (Pew Pew Tactical)
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** Live video, 360-degree rotation, host can show bore, frame markings, bluing under light on request. The chat asks, the host shows.

### 5. Scams + no real buyer protection (GunBroker $400 cap, Proxibid/HiBid no rating system)
- **Category:** Scam / Fraud Risk / Platform Indifference
- **Frequency:** 10 | **Intensity:** 9 | **Score: 90**
- **Who feels it most:** Every buyer. Especially Marcus (high-ticket) and Dale.
- **The problem:** GunBroker Buyer's Protection caps coverage at $400 — meaningless on a $2-$8K vintage purchase. Account takeovers ("the guy was able to hack someone else's GB account and use their good rating in the fraud") are widespread. Proxibid has no buyer rating system; HiBid lets sellers see max bids (encouraging shill bidding). Recourse is essentially zero.
- **Evidence:**
  - "Their protection policy that they have is only good for $400 so it's worthless!" (Sniper's Hide, 2024)
  - "You have zero recourse when you get scammed and on top of that they will lock your account." (Multiple GunBroker complaint threads)
  - "Gun broker may or may not try to help. In my case...they didn't do anything and I actually saw the scammer back on with same item about a week later." (Sniper's Hide, July 2024)
  - "Based on my experience Gunbrokers.com has NO BUYER'S PROTECTION and I can not recommend that anyone use this site." (Complaints Board)
  - "There is no 'claims process'. There is only a black hole into which complaints disappear forever." (Colt Forum)
  - "Proxibid lets scammers and fraudulent sellers use their site and has no rating or review system, making it easy for a person to unknowingly purchase from a scammer." (Ripoff Report)
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** Live commerce = real-time identity, real-time inventory verification, vetted FFL dealers only as hosts. Account-takeover scams structurally impossible because the seller is a verified FFL on camera, live.

### 6. Customer service: nobody answers, no human to call, support is "a black hole"
- **Category:** Platform Support
- **Frequency:** 10 | **Intensity:** 8 | **Score: 80**
- **Who feels it most:** Every buyer on legacy platforms
- **The problem:** GunBroker offers no phone support; live chat only for paid subscribers ($50). Email responses are canned. Proxibid sides with the auctioneer. HiBid hides behind "we're just a platform."
- **Evidence:**
  - "I've been trying to contact a human there for over 30 days with zero response despite a dozen emails as well as written correspondence... Right now I think it's all smoke and mirrors." (1919 A4 Forums)
  - "They only offer a live chat (if you subscribe for 50 bucks) and zero phone support." (1919 A4 Forums)
  - "There is really no way to get ahold of any live body from what I can tell." (Colt Forum)
  - "The canned responses from GunBroker are so frustrating, and you can't speak to anyone at GunBroker I'm left to share this information on forums." (SmartCustomer)
  - "Their support is slow, unhelpful, and not interested in resolving things fairly, and instead of protecting both sides, they punish loyal buyers." (Proxibid)
  - "HiBid assumes no responsibility and will not intervene, even if the seller is a scammer." (HiBid ComplaintsBoard)
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** Live-commerce platform with in-stream chat moderation, post-show ticket support, and a vetted-dealer model (the dealer is accountable as a licensee, not just a username).

## Tier 2 — Daily Grind (Score 50-74)

### 7. NFA dealer goes silent during the wait period
- **Category:** NFA Wait-Time Customer Service
- **Frequency:** 8 | **Intensity:** 9 | **Score: 72**
- **Who feels it most:** Trent (NFA Buyer)
- **The problem:** ATF wait times collapsed in 2025-2026 (4 days for individual eForm 4, 18 days for trust). The bottleneck moved to the dealer. Buyers report "28 days of delay from law enforcement notification without explanation" and dealers refusing to escalate.
- **Evidence:**
  - "28 days of delay from law enforcement notification without explanation." (Silencer Central BBB complaint)
  - "Over a month from ordering and paying before Silencer Central submitted their information." (Hunt Talk thread)
  - "Cynthia... refused to escalate to management." (Trustpilot Silencer Central review)
  - "Over 3 months of back-order time and three weeks without shipping information after receiving devices from the manufacturer." (Silencer Central BBB)
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** Live shows are a dealer-accountability channel. Trent can verify on stream that the dealer is active, responsive, and moving inventory. Pre-purchase trust signal that no static dealer page can match.

### 8. The deal-hunter fee stack: $35 ship + $50 transfer + $42 tax + $8 comp fee = "no deal"
- **Category:** Total Cost of Ownership / Pricing
- **Frequency:** 9 | **Intensity:** 7 | **Score: 63**
- **Who feels it most:** Kyle (Deal-Hunter)
- **The problem:** The advertised price on r/GunDeals is the floor, not the ceiling. By the time he checks out, the $400 deal is $535, and his local shop had it for $479 without a 4-day shipping wait.
- **Evidence:**
  - "FFL transfer fees vary wildly, with some charging as little as $15 and others asking $75 or more for the exact same process." (UGS FFL Fees Guide)
  - "Prices can vary by $30 or more in the same zip code, so spending five minutes on the phone can literally save you enough to buy an extra box of ammo." (UGS)
  - "Be aware that online purchases come with add-on fees for shipping and FFL transfer." (NRA Family)
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** All-in pricing shown live. No surprise fees. Lower platform fees + dealer ships from inventory the buyer can see live.

### 9. FFL refuses the transfer or requires only FFL-shipped items
- **Category:** Transfer Friction / Local Dealer Behavior
- **Frequency:** 8 | **Intensity:** 8 | **Score: 64**
- **Who feels it most:** Hank (Rural), Dale, Kyle — any buyer routing a transfer
- **The problem:** Local FFLs increasingly refuse private-party transfers; some incorrectly cite "interstate commerce concerns" that aren't legal issues. Buyer has to find a second FFL — often a longer drive.
- **Evidence:**
  - "Pistols have arrived at gun shops with buyers who had done several transfers in the past, but the shops refused to transfer the gun because it didn't come from another FFL." (Smith & Wesson Forum)
  - "Some FFLs incorrectly claim that firearms shipped from private parties are 'in transfer jail' and at high risk of being sent back due to interstate commerce laws and ATF concerns, despite the sender having prior experience with over 50 out-of-state transfers." (Sniper's Hide Forum)
  - "Reason is usually ignorance on the part of the receiving FFL, as most of them are incredibly ignorant of the laws." (Sniper's Hide)
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** Vetted FFL-to-FFL network. Platform-routed transfers from dealers that have already accepted the transfer chain. Buyer knows in advance the transfer will complete.

### 10. The 90-minute drive for any real selection
- **Category:** Distance to FFL / Local Inventory Drought
- **Frequency:** 7 | **Intensity:** 8 | **Score: 56**
- **Who feels it most:** Hank (Rural Buyer)
- **The problem:** Rural buyers face a structural inventory drought. The one local FFL has 12 SKUs. Driving 90 minutes for a real selection is the only option. After purchase: drive back for the transfer pickup. Three drives, one gun.
- **Evidence:**
  - "If you have limited inventory but still operate a storefront and play the 'I will/can order it' game you will fail." (Glock Talk — choosing inventory thread)
  - "Having a lot of new inventory sitting on the shelves is a good way to not make payroll." (Glock Talk)
  - "FFLs don't contact customers when shipments arrive and then tell customers they're too busy to process transfers immediately, with customers waiting extended periods." (FFL Dealer Network reviews — referenced in May dealer research)
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** National inventory from his couch. 5,000+ SKUs every week from dealers across the country. Single round trip to his local FFL for pickup — not three drives.

### 11. Auction "buyer's premium" sticker shock + walk-aways
- **Category:** Auction Fees
- **Frequency:** 8 | **Intensity:** 7 | **Score: 56**
- **Who feels it most:** Dale, Marcus
- **The problem:** Industry-standard 15-25% buyer's premium is a known cost, but cumulative with packing, shipping, insurance, and transfer it makes the auction model lose competitiveness against direct-from-dealer purchase. Bidders walk away mid-auction rather than overpay.
- **Evidence:**
  - "Bidders should be prepared to walk away if the price exceeds their limits." (Multiple auction guides)
  - "When you ignore fees, you might win an auction only to discover you can't afford the final total." (William George blog)
  - Rock Island: 17.5% Premier + 1% RIAC Live + 3.5% credit card service fee
  - HiBid: 18% buyer's premium common
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** Flat platform fee structure, no buyer's premium. Sticker price is closer to the out-the-door price.

### 12. Sellers ignore questions, then ignore post-auction communication
- **Category:** Seller Communication
- **Frequency:** 7 | **Intensity:** 8 | **Score: 56**
- **Who feels it most:** Every buyer on GunBroker
- **The problem:** Post-auction, the seller has 5 days to respond. Buyers report waiting longer with no contact. GunBroker provides the seller's phone number after win, but enforcement is nil.
- **Evidence:**
  - "Won a Browning 725 auction, paid via Venmo as the seller requested, provided FFL dealer info, but then never received tracking information." (Forum aggregate)
  - "Seller Does Not Respond after I Won an Auction" — entire GunBroker help category exists for this
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** Vetted FFL dealers only. Post-show fulfillment is a service-level commitment, not a private-seller hope.

## Tier 3 — Chronic Friction (Score 30-49)

### 13. The local shop's selection is the same 12 guns it had 6 months ago
- **Category:** Local Inventory Drought
- **Frequency:** 8 | **Intensity:** 5 | **Score: 40**
- **Who feels it most:** Hank, Kyle, Jen/Jeff
- **The problem:** Small-town and suburban shops can't afford to hold deep inventory ("Having a lot of new inventory sitting on the shelves is a good way to not make payroll"). The buyer is left with the same wall, week after week.
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** Live national inventory drops — new SKUs daily across the dealer network.

### 14. Counterfeit / fraudulent lots on platforms with no rating system
- **Category:** Scam Risk / Platform Trust
- **Frequency:** 6 | **Intensity:** 8 | **Score: 48**
- **Who feels it most:** Dale (Proxibid/HiBid buyer)
- **The problem:** Proxibid and HiBid hide behind being "just a platform" and don't verify sellers or provide buyer ratings. Counterfeit goods (including counterfeit coins, watches, and arguably misrepresented firearms) slip through.
- **Evidence:** "Proxibid lets scammers and fraudulent sellers use their site and has no rating or review system." (Ripoff Report)
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** Vetted FFL hosts only; live show inventory shown on camera, not staged photos.

### 15. The "compliance fee" added at checkout (not the seller's fee, the platform's)
- **Category:** Pricing Opacity (sub-pain of #2)
- **Frequency:** 7 | **Intensity:** 6 | **Score: 42**
- **Who feels it most:** Kyle, Marcus
- **The problem:** "The objection is that it hides a fee. That charge should be part of the seller's fees, not added on at the end."
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** Transparent all-in fee structure.

### 16. Misrepresented condition descriptions ("minimal use," "meticulously serviced")
- **Category:** Listing Quality
- **Frequency:** 7 | **Intensity:** 6 | **Score: 42**
- **Who feels it most:** Marcus, Dale
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** Live host inspection, on-stream condition Q&A.

### 17. Local FFL closed before buyer can pick up the transfer
- **Category:** Pickup Logistics
- **Frequency:** 6 | **Intensity:** 6 | **Score: 36**
- **Who feels it most:** Hank, Kyle
- **The problem:** Local FFL closes at 5. Buyer works until 6. Pickup is Saturday only.
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** Pre-show 4473 prep, after-hours pickup windows at participating dealers.

### 18. Discovery problem: "where do I even find the kind of inventory I want?"
- **Category:** Inventory Discovery
- **Frequency:** 7 | **Intensity:** 5 | **Score: 35**
- **Who feels it most:** Marcus (vintage), Dale (estate)
- **The problem:** Estate lots, consignment, and rare collector pieces are scattered across regional auction houses, GunBroker, Forgotten Weapons partner channels, and word-of-mouth. There's no single feed.
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** Daily aggregated live shows across vetted dealers — a single watchlist for collectors.

### 19. The compliance / state law trap (CA, NY, NJ, MA, IL feature laws)
- **Category:** State Compliance Trap
- **Frequency:** 6 | **Intensity:** 7 | **Score: 42**
- **Who feels it most:** Buyers in restrictive states; sellers shipping into them
- **Evidence:** "One buyer won an auction that included about a dozen preban magazines but Rock Island refused to ship them to Massachusetts." (Northeast Shooters)
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** State-aware checkout — flags featureless / mag-cap / FOID / permit issues before bidding.

### 20. The "I just want to lurk first" friction
- **Category:** First-Timer Friction
- **Frequency:** 6 | **Intensity:** 6 | **Score: 36**
- **Who feels it most:** Jen/Jeff (First-Timer)
- **The problem:** Walking into a brick-and-mortar dealer when you're new is intimidating. Most online channels assume you already know what you want.
- **GunStreamer feature solving it:** Anonymous lurk-friendly live show. Watch 5 streams, ask one question, signal-up when ready.
