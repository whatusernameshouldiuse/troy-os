# Product Launch Checklist

**Version:** 1.0
**Created:** 2026-02-03
**Purpose:** Reusable checklist for launching any product or major feature across Troy's projects

---

## How to Use This Checklist

1. Copy this template when starting a new launch
2. Delete sections that don't apply
3. Add project-specific tasks as needed
4. Track completion dates as you go

---

## T-30 Days: Foundation

### Strategy & Positioning
- [ ] Define the ONE thing we're launching (clear scope)
- [ ] Identify primary ICP for this launch
- [ ] Write the core message (one sentence: problem → solution)
- [ ] Define success metrics (what does "good" look like?)
- [ ] Identify 3 key differentiators from alternatives

### Content Creation
- [ ] Draft landing page copy (hero, benefits, social proof, CTA)
- [ ] Draft launch email sequence (3-5 emails)
- [ ] Create social media content calendar (launch week)
- [ ] Write FAQ document (anticipate 10 common questions)
- [ ] Prepare visual assets list (what graphics/photos needed?)

### Technical Prep
- [ ] Confirm product/feature is functional (test end-to-end)
- [ ] Set up tracking (analytics, UTMs, conversion events)
- [ ] Create landing page (or product page)
- [ ] Set up email flows in Klaviyo (or email tool)
- [ ] Test checkout/signup flow

---

## T-14 Days: Build & Test

### Marketing Assets Live
- [ ] Landing page live and tested
- [ ] Email sequence built and tested (send test emails)
- [ ] Social posts written and scheduled
- [ ] Any paid ad campaigns created (not launched)
- [ ] Affiliate/partner materials ready (if applicable)

### Outreach & PR
- [ ] Identify 10-20 people to tell personally (warm outreach list)
- [ ] Draft personal outreach messages
- [ ] Identify any influencers/partners for co-promotion
- [ ] Prepare press/PR pitch (if applicable)

### Operations
- [ ] Customer support prepared (FAQ, response templates)
- [ ] Fulfillment process confirmed (for physical products)
- [ ] Team roles clear (who handles what on launch day)

---

## T-7 Days: Final Prep

### Review & Polish
- [ ] Final review of landing page (typos, broken links, mobile)
- [ ] Final review of email sequence (preview all emails)
- [ ] Test all CTAs and links
- [ ] Confirm tracking is working (fire test conversion)

### Pre-Launch Buzz
- [ ] Teaser content goes out (if building anticipation)
- [ ] Email list primed (mention launch is coming)
- [ ] Personal outreach to warm list ("hey, launching next week...")

### Contingency
- [ ] Document known issues and workarounds
- [ ] Backup contact info for critical services (hosting, payment, etc.)
- [ ] Define "abort" criteria (when to pull the plug if something breaks)

---

## T-1 Day: Pre-Launch

- [ ] Final check: landing page loads fast
- [ ] Final check: checkout/signup works
- [ ] Final check: email tool connected and ready
- [ ] Pre-schedule launch day social posts
- [ ] Prepare launch day announcement email
- [ ] Get good sleep

---

## Launch Day (T-0)

### Morning (6-9 AM)
- [ ] Publish launch blog post (if applicable)
- [ ] Send launch email to list
- [ ] Post launch announcement on social (all channels)
- [ ] Personal outreach to VIP list ("we're live!")

### Midday (12-2 PM)
- [ ] Check initial metrics (opens, clicks, traffic)
- [ ] Respond to any comments/questions
- [ ] Post follow-up content (social proof, early reactions)
- [ ] Address any technical issues immediately

### Evening (5-8 PM)
- [ ] Share early traction (numbers, testimonials, screenshots)
- [ ] Engage with launch day comments
- [ ] Post "thank you" content if hitting milestones
- [ ] Document any issues for Day 2 fix

---

## T+1 to T+7: Launch Week

### Day 1 (T+1)
- [ ] Send "Day 1 results" email (social proof)
- [ ] Address any overnight issues
- [ ] Continue social engagement
- [ ] Capture early testimonials

### Day 2-3
- [ ] Publish launch week content (case study, behind-the-scenes)
- [ ] Follow up with anyone who engaged but didn't convert
- [ ] Collect and share customer feedback
- [ ] Start retargeting (if running paid)

### Day 4-5
- [ ] Mid-week email (new angle or objection handler)
- [ ] Compile early success stories
- [ ] Address common questions publicly

### Day 6-7
- [ ] "Last chance" or "one week later" email
- [ ] Compile launch week metrics
- [ ] Prepare for steady-state marketing

---

## T+14: Post-Launch Review

### Performance Analysis
- [ ] Review all launch metrics against goals
- [ ] Identify what worked best (top content, channels, messages)
- [ ] Identify what didn't work (low performers, technical issues)
- [ ] Document lessons learned

### Optimization
- [ ] Update landing page based on feedback
- [ ] Refine email sequence based on performance
- [ ] Adjust targeting based on who actually converted
- [ ] Create ongoing nurture flow for non-converters

### Systemize
- [ ] Save winning content as templates
- [ ] Update this checklist with project-specific learnings
- [ ] Document any new SOPs created during launch

## Launch Types: What to Emphasize

### Product Launch (New to Market)
- Heavy on education (what is it, how it works)
- Focus on early adopter benefits
- Build waitlist → controlled launch
- Collect testimonials aggressively

### Feature Launch (Existing Product)
- Lead with the problem it solves
- Show before/after
- Target existing customers first
- Upsell/cross-sell angle

### Content Launch (Course, Book, Resource)
- Heavy pre-launch content (excerpts, teasers)
- Limited-time bonuses
- Social proof from beta/early readers
- Clear transformation promise

### Service Launch
- Lead with credibility/authority
- Case studies critical
- Offer introductory pricing or limited slots
- Personal outreach to ideal clients

---

## Metrics to Track

### Pre-Launch
- Email list growth (signups for launch notification)
- Landing page traffic and conversion
- Social engagement on teaser content

### Launch Week
- Email open/click rates
- Landing page conversion rate
- Revenue/signups (the goal metric)
- Customer acquisition cost (if paid ads)

### Post-Launch
- Customer satisfaction (NPS, reviews, feedback)
- Retention/churn (for subscriptions)
- Referral rate
- Lifetime value indicators

---

## Red Flags: When to Pause

Stop and reassess if:
- [ ] Checkout/signup is broken (fix before continuing)
- [ ] Email tool not sending (critical)
- [ ] Major negative feedback emerging (address before promoting more)
- [ ] Traffic but zero conversions (messaging problem)
- [ ] Technical issues affecting experience
